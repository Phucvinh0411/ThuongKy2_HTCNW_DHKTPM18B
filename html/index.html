Introduction - Contact

Flowering plants are plants which produce flowers as their reproductive sructures. They come in a great variety grasses, sedges, rushes, many trees, as well as familiar plants like daffodils and daises. Flowers often, though not always, have brightly coloured petals. The flowers conceal inside them the female ovules which will be fertilised by pollen grains. The pollen grains are the male parts of the flower. Fertilised ovules develop into the seeds from which new plants will grow. The fact that the ovules are protected inside flowers has contributed greatly to the success of flowering plants.
